Research 02 · Earth Analogs

Cryogenic Carbonate Lakes

Winter freeze-out drives alkaline lakes of the Cariboo Plateau toward amorphous Mg–Ca carbonate saturation — ice as both piston and shield. A terrestrial window onto how Mars made magnesite.

About

Lingqi (Leo) Zeng

Add
Portrait

I'm a postdoctoral researcher in Briony Horgan's group at Purdue University's Department of Earth, Atmospheric and Planetary Sciences, working at the intersection of orbital remote sensing, geochemical modeling, and Earth analog fieldwork.

My research asks a single question from several directions: what happened to the carbon on early Mars? I build CRISM hyperspectral detection pipelines, run PHREEQC Pitzer-model simulations of carbonate precipitation, and study cryogenic alkaline lakes on Earth as analogs for how — and whether — a wet planet stays habitable.
